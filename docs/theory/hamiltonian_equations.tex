\section*{Total Hamiltonian}

We consider a coupled molecular--electromagnetic system with total Hamiltonian
\begin{equation}
    H_{\text{tot}} = H_{\text{MD}} + H_{\text{EM}} + H_{\text{int}}.
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Molecular Hamiltonian $H_{\text{MD}}$}

In classical molecular dynamics with force-field parameters, the molecular Hamiltonian is
\begin{equation}
    H_{\text{MD}} = \sum_{i} \frac{\mathbf{p}_i^2}{2 m_i}
    + \sum_{\text{bonds}} U_{\text{bond}}
    + \sum_{\text{angles}} U_{\text{angle}}
    + \sum_{\text{dihedrals}} U_{\text{dihedral}}
    + \sum_{i<j} \left( U_{\text{LJ}}(r_{ij}) + U_{\text{Coul}}(r_{ij}) \right),
\end{equation}
where $m_i$ and $\mathbf{p}_i$ are the mass and momentum of atom $i$.

\subsection*{Electromagnetic Hamiltonian $H_{\text{EM}}$}

In the absence of free charges and currents, the electromagnetic Hamiltonian in a
dispersive medium is
\begin{equation}
    H_{\text{EM}} = \frac{1}{2} \int_V
    \left(
        \mathbf{E}(\mathbf{r}, t) \cdot \mathbf{D}(\mathbf{r}, t)
        +
        \mathbf{B}(\mathbf{r}, t) \cdot \mathbf{H}(\mathbf{r}, t)
    \right)
    \, dV.
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Interaction Hamiltonian $H_{\text{int}}$}

Within the electric dipole approximation, the light--matter interaction is
\begin{equation}
    H_{\text{int}}(t) = - \sum_{m} \bm{\mu}_m \cdot
    \mathbf{E}(\mathbf{r}_m(t), t),
\end{equation}
